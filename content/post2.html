post #2: going left, going right

ah, navigation, what I suppose is the primarily reason why content management systems are so popular.

I'm a minimalist by nature. so I feel like arrows are sufficient. I don't even feel like menu/list styled nav bars are necessary. after all, this is a page for web experiments. I think it'd be fun to explore simply by going right, sequentially. It's not large enough to warrant a switch to something that looks like a portfolio. I'm probably going to look at things here as pathetic little toys in a few months, but hey, that's part of taking ownership of your code, right?

in other news, I just finished implementing my Rust raytracer based on Peter Shirley's first book in his Raytracing series, and am very exciting to move on with the rest. I'm curious if I can import it into web assembly and have it work on the browser? that should be fun.

I like Rust. I won't necessarily hop on the 'better C++' train, but I do think writing Rust code every once in a while makes you a better C++ programmer. the compiler essentially marks in you, sometimes with molten metal, good programming habits. I hope there'll be opportunities to use it more in my career, aside from hobby projects.
